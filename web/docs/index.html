Welcome to Tagwise

The Telegram bot that tracks top Polymarket traders and lets you copy their moves automatically.

What is Tagwise?

Tagwise is a Telegram bot that gives you a direct edge on Polymarket — the world's largest prediction market. It monitors top traders in real time, sends you instant alerts when they make moves, and lets you copy their trades with one tap.

Whether you're tracking political markets, sports events, crypto predictions, or tech outcomes, Tagwise surfaces the smartest money so you can trade with conviction.

How It Works

Tagwise runs three services behind the scenes to keep you ahead of the market:

  • Bot Service — handles all your Telegram interactions: commands, menus, alerts, and copy trading.
  • Worker Service — polls tracked wallets for new trades every 30 seconds, then pushes real-time alerts to your chat.
  • Leaderboard Service — refreshes top trader rankings every 6 hours across all categories and time periods.

You interact with everything through Telegram — no extra apps, no browser extensions, no dashboards to check.

Security

How Tagwise protects your data and funds.

Non-Custodial Design

Tagwise is built with a non-custodial architecture. This means the bot never holds or controls your funds directly. When you create a trading wallet through Tagwise, the private key is encrypted and stored securely — it's never accessible in plaintext to anyone, including the Tagwise team.

All trades are executed on-chain through the Polymarket Builder API, meaning every transaction is verifiable and transparent on the blockchain.

Wallet Custody via Privy

Trading wallets created through Tagwise are secured using Privy, a non-custodial embedded wallet infrastructure provider. Here's how it works:

  • Key Generation — when you create a trading wallet, a new Ethereum-compatible keypair is generated and stored inside Privy's Trusted Execution Environment (TEE) — a hardware-isolated secure enclave.
  • Zero-Access Storage — your private key never leaves the TEE in plaintext. Neither Tagwise nor Privy's own servers can read it directly.
  • Signing on Demand — when a trade needs to be executed, the signing request is sent to Privy's TEE. Only the resulting cryptographic signature is returned — the key itself stays inside the enclave.
Important
Your trading wallet is separate from your personal Polymarket account. Only deposit the amount you want to use for copy trading. Never deposit more than you're comfortable risking.

Data Privacy

Tagwise stores the minimum data needed to operate:

  • Telegram user ID — to route alerts and manage your account.
  • Tracked wallet addresses — public on-chain addresses (not private keys of wallets you track).
  • Trading wallet — encrypted keypair for copy trading (only if you create one).
  • Subscription status — to manage free/PRO tier access.

Tagwise does not collect your name, email, phone number, or any personal information beyond your Telegram user ID.

Payment Security

All payments are processed through NOWPayments, a third-party crypto payment processor. Tagwise never handles payment data directly. NOWPayments supports various cryptocurrencies and handles all transaction verification.

When you subscribe to PRO, NOWPayments generates a payment address, you send crypto, and once confirmed on-chain, your PRO status is activated automatically.

Tip
You can check your subscription status anytime with the /account command. It shows your current plan, expiry date, and wallet limits.

Your First Trade

Follow along to go from zero to your first copy trade.

Step by Step

Here's the complete flow from opening the bot to executing your first copy trade:

Browse the Leaderboard

Type /toptraders to open the leaderboard. Select a category (e.g., Politics, Crypto) and a time period (Daily, Weekly, Monthly, or All-Time). You'll see a ranked list of the top-performing wallets with key stats.

Track a Top Trader

Tap on a trader from the leaderboard to view their full stats — PnL, ROI, win rate, and recent trades. Hit the Track button to start following their wallet. You'll now receive alerts whenever they make a trade.

Create Your Trading Wallet

Use /wallet to create your on-chain trading wallet. Tagwise will generate a new wallet and show you the address. Send USDC to this address — this is the balance you'll trade with.

Wait for an Alert

When a tracked wallet makes a trade, you'll receive an instant Telegram notification with the market name, the side they're buying (Yes/No), the amount, and the current price.

Copy the Trade

Each alert includes a Copy Trade button. Tap it to mirror the exact position. Or, enable auto copy with /copy to have all trades from a specific wallet copied automatically at your chosen position size.

Risk Warning
Copy trading involves risk. Past performance of tracked wallets does not guarantee future results. Only trade with funds you can afford to lose. Start with small position sizes to get familiar with how copy trading works.

Understanding Alerts

Trade alerts include the following information:

  • Wallet — the tracked wallet that made the trade (with a short address label).
  • Market — the Polymarket event being traded (e.g., "Will Bitcoin hit $100k by March 2026?").
  • Side — whether they bought Yes or No.
  • Amount — the USDC value of their position.
  • Price — the entry price (e.g., $0.67 per share).
  • Action buttons — Copy Trade, View Wallet Stats, Untrack.

Auto vs. Manual Copy

Tagwise supports two copy trading modes:

Mode How It Works Best For
Manual Copy Get an alert → tap "Copy Trade" → confirm Selective traders who want to review each trade
Auto Copy Trades are mirrored automatically at your set position size Hands-off traders who trust a wallet's strategy

Copy Trading

Automatically mirror trades from wallets you trust.

Creating a Trading Wallet

Before you can copy trade, you need to create an on-chain trading wallet. This is a dedicated wallet managed by Tagwise for executing trades on your behalf.

/wallet

This generates a new Ethereum-compatible wallet, encrypts the private key with Fernet encryption, and displays your wallet address. Send USDC to this address to fund your trading balance.

Only deposit what you're willing to risk
Your trading wallet is separate from your personal wallets. Only send the amount you intend to use for copy trading. All trades carry risk.

How Copy Trading Works

When you copy a trade, here's what happens under the hood:

  1. Tagwise detects a new trade from a tracked wallet.
  2. If auto-copy is enabled for that wallet, the bot prepares an identical order.
  3. The order is submitted through the Polymarket Builder API, which handles on-chain execution.
  4. Your trading wallet's private key is decrypted momentarily to sign the transaction, then immediately discarded from memory.
  5. You receive a confirmation alert with the trade details and your updated balance.

Setting Position Sizes

You control how much to allocate per copied trade. Use the /copy command to configure:

  • Fixed amount — set a flat USDC amount per trade (e.g., $10 per copy).
  • Proportional — mirror the same percentage of your balance as the trader uses.
/copy 0x1234...abcd 10

This enables auto-copy on the specified wallet with a $10 position size per trade.

Multi-Buy Alerts PRO

Detect when multiple tracked wallets converge on the same trade.

What Are Multi-Buy Alerts?

Multi-buy alerts fire when multiple tracked wallets buy the same side of the same market within a short time window. This is one of the most powerful signals Tagwise offers — when several independent top traders converge on the same position, it often indicates strong conviction.

How Detection Works

Tagwise's worker service continuously monitors all tracked wallets. When it detects that two or more wallets have taken the same side on a market, it triggers a multi-buy alert. The alert includes:

  • Market name — which event they're trading
  • Side — Yes or No
  • Number of wallets — how many tracked wallets are on the same side
  • Wallet list — which specific wallets made the trades
  • Time window — how quickly they converged

Signal Strength

Multi-buy alerts are labeled by signal strength based on the number of converging wallets:

Wallets Signal Description
2 Moderate Two wallets agree — worth noting
3-4 Strong Clear consensus forming among multiple traders
5+ Very Strong Rare convergence — high conviction signal

Consensus

The real power of multi-buy alerts is consensus detection. When unrelated top traders independently reach the same conclusion, it's a meaningful signal that goes beyond any single trader's edge.

PRO Only
Multi-buy alerts are exclusive to PRO subscribers. On the Free tier, you'll still receive individual trade alerts for tracked wallets.

Confidence Scoring PRO

AI-powered scoring of trade quality based on trader history.

How Scoring Works

Every trade alert from a tracked wallet includes a confidence score — a 0-100 rating that reflects how likely the trade is to be profitable based on the trader's historical performance in similar markets.

The score is calculated using several factors:

  • Historical win rate — the trader's overall success rate on resolved positions.
  • Category performance — how well the trader performs in this specific market category (e.g., Politics vs. Sports).
  • Position sizing — larger positions relative to the trader's typical size suggest higher conviction.
  • Recency — recent performance is weighted more heavily than old results.
  • Volume context — traders with more history get more reliable scores.

Score Ranges

Score Label Meaning
80-100 High Confidence Strong historical edge in this type of market
60-79 Medium Confidence Decent track record, moderate expected edge
40-59 Low Confidence Limited history or mixed results in this category
0-39 Very Low New trader or poor category performance

Filtering by Score

PRO users can set a minimum confidence threshold for auto-copy. For example, you can configure auto-copy to only execute when the confidence score is above 70 — skipping low-conviction trades automatically.

This is especially useful when tracking many wallets at once. Rather than copying every trade, you let the confidence scoring system filter for the highest-quality signals.

Recommended Strategy
Start with a confidence threshold of 70+ and lower it if you're not getting enough trades. Combine with multi-buy alerts for the strongest possible signals.
